How I got it, what I've learned, and what's next

My Very First Internship

// First, Some Background

Let me set the scene here. At the time, I was a second year CSE (Computer Science & Engineering) undergraduate at UC Davis. I spend much of my free time getting tunnel vision from reading r/cscareerquestions, and the rest of the time trying to figure how to get good grades, do meaningful personal projects, and uphold my responsibilities as a club officer for various clubs on campus (shout-out to BCNC and DCSC). It's no easy task but it definitely kept me busy.

At the time of applying to Genentech, there wasn't much that I could show off. Sure, I had an impressive resume during high school, but that was relative. The entire game has changed ever since I came to UC Davis. I had a python web scraper that I whipped up in a day or so, and some random C++ programs from school. I also went to HackDavis and put a project I made on there on my resume, but none of the projects took more than a full day of development.

But here's the biggest plot twist of all, none of the work I did at school had a direct relationship as to how I got the internship. So how did I get the position? Networking.

It was well into Spring (March), when someone I knew messaged me to apply to Genentech, and that she could get me an interview. This opportunity was the light at the end of a very dark tunnel of many, many rejections from other companies. With the little hope that I had left, I decided to apply, and see what happens next.

// Next Steps

After applying, I wasn't sure what to expect. This would have been the first time that I've ever applied through a referral system. I've always heard of people getting referred for internships, but I never really knew how to get one (this is especially difficult for trying to get into a company where you don't know anyone). I suppose I lucked out because next thing you know, I was scheduled for an on-site interview with my future manger.

I won't go into too many details about the interview, but I will say that it was a non-technical for a role titled: "Biologics Drug Substance Manufacturing IT" over at Vacaville.
